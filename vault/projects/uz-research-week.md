Designed and presented an automatic seed sowing robot at the University of Zimbabwe Virtual Research, Innovation and Industrialization Week 2021 ("UZ on the Move: Seeding Industry", 23–27 August 2021), published in the official Book of Abstracts.

Conventional seed sowing in Zimbabwe is slow, labour-intensive and costly. The robot mechanises the process using low-cost components that can be built in locally available workshops — an eco-friendly, energy-efficient approach to smart agriculture.

- Arduino microcontroller with a BTS7960 motor driver, geared DC motors and a 12V battery on a two-wheel-drive chassis.
- Seed metering via a scotch yoke mechanism, converting rotary motor motion into linear slider motion.
- Remote control from an Android phone over Bluetooth.
